The Chop Serve

For this serve, you need to use your normal grip. Stand on the side of the Table, and be relaxed. Once you throw your ball around 16cm up the air, swing the racket in a slicing motion while having your racket tilted upward. Make sure to aim to hit the ball when it is already low and to only brush against it. If the contact is thinner, you will produce more backspin.




The Pendulum Serve

For this serve, you need to use your normal grip. Stand sideways to the Table, and bend your knees. Once the ball is up 16cm in the air, swing the racket from left to right in a circular motion as your racket's tip points slightly downwards. Make sure to hit the ball at its' lower right side.




The Shovel Serve

For this serve you need to use your normal grip. Be sure to stand square to the table. Once the ball is up 16cm in the air, swing the racket from down to up while the face is slightly open like you are shoveling or scooping something. For this serve, you need to hit the lower-left side of the ball. Do not use too much arm or else the serve will not work.
